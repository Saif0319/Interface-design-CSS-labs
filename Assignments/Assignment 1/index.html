A Brief Article On Georgia Font

Origin

G eorgia is a typeface designed by typographer Matthew Carter in 1993. It is another of the Roman typefaces with a star Serif, which have been part of the history of graphic design. It was founded to work with Microsoft in the 90s era. His peculiar American name, It is intended for a small board that the designer found in the state of Georgia (USA). The naming has provided even more visualization to the typeface, so Microsoft decided to register it.

Design

Georgia has been described as having a typographic personality â€” even called friendly and intimate. Since the typeface is still legible at low resolutions, it creates an old-world charm with a modern appeal for online designs. Georgia is a bit more formal than some of the more common sans serif fonts. However, it's not so formal as to look old school. If you are looking for a font that is a bit more regal, but not stiff, then this is the one you definitely must look at. It is a font that offers a much more serious and professional look on web pages or content that requires running text or big headlines.

Where It's Used

  • Georgia is specifically created for on-screen use,
  • A wide category of books and publishers that have opted for this typography due to its high readability range.
  • Lots of big-name newspapers like the Guardian, New York Times Times, Telegraph, Wall Street Journal and the Independent use Georgia font
  • Georgia is also used in large posters and in communication media where large formats are required.
